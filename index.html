Ohio Notice Tracker
3-day, 30-day, and other required notices
▼
Ohio Notice Requirements:
• 3-Day Notice — For nonpayment of rent. Must give 3 days to pay or vacate before filing eviction.
• 30-Day Notice — To end a month-to-month tenancy. Must be given 30 days before next rent due date.
• Lease Violations — 30-day notice with option to cure for lease violations.
• Unconditional Quit — For illegal activity or serious damage, no cure period required.

Ohio Landlord Reminders:
• Security deposit: no cap on amount, but must return within 30 days of move-out with itemized list of deductions.
• Ohio R.C. 5321: Landlord must maintain property in habitable condition (heat, water, plumbing, electrical, structural).
• Late fees: must be in the lease to be enforceable. Ohio has no statutory limit but must be "reasonable."
• Eviction: MUST go through court. Self-help evictions (changing locks, shutting off utilities) are illegal in Ohio.
• Lead paint disclosure required for pre-1978 properties (federal law).
